Tokopedia Seller Onboarding

Reducing Time-to-First-Sale by 35% through Progressive Disclosure and UX Optimization.

The Problem

New sellers on Tokopedia were dropping off at a alarming rate of 60% during the registration process. User research revealed that the forms were intimidatingly long, asking for sensitive banking details before the user even listed a single product.

60% Drop-off rate at initial sign-up stage.

The Strategy

I proposed a "Progressive Onboarding" strategy. Instead of front-loading all data collection, we allow sellers to list their first item immediately. We only ask for verification details when they receive their first order—when motivation is highest.

Workflow Diagram Placeholder

Key changes implemented:

  • Removed 4 redundant fields from initial sign-up.
  • Added a "Completion Meter" to gamify the profile setup.
  • Created automated email nudges for incomplete profiles.

Impact

Based on usability testing with 15 potential sellers, the redesign showed significant improvements in key metrics.

35% Faster Time-to-First-Listing
8/10 User Satisfaction Score

CampusSwap

A hyper-local P2P marketplace designed exclusively for university students to trade textbooks and gear safely.

Market Opportunity

Students spend an average of Rp 2.000.000/semester on textbooks that they only use for 6 months. Existing solutions like Facebook Marketplace or general e-commerce sites are riddled with scams, lack campus verification, and require expensive shipping.

The Solution

CampusSwap creates a closed-loop marketplace where users must verify their Student ID (.ac.id email) to trade. This creates high trust and enables hyper-local meetups on campus, eliminating shipping costs.

Value Proposition

"Trade safely with classmates you can trust, right on campus."

Go-To-Market Strategy

We plan to launch using a hyper-local strategy, focusing on density rather than breadth.

  • Phase 1 (Pilot): Launch at 1 University (e.g., ITS/UI) using Campus Ambassadors program.
  • Phase 2 (Trust): Partner with Student Executive Boards (BEM) for official endorsement.
  • Monetization: Freemium model. Free listing for basics, small fee for "Urgent" highlight or verified textbook guarantee.

The Challenge

PT Aneka Coffee Industry was relying on paper-based forms for critical factory audits. This legacy process meant data entry took 3 days per audit, leading to delayed decision-making on quality control and lost data.

My Role & Execution

I acted as the bridge between the Quality Control department (Users) and the IT Department (Engineers). My responsibilities included:

  • Gathering requirements through user interviews with field auditors.
  • Creating low-fidelity wireframes in Figma to validate flows before coding.
  • Prioritizing features for the MVP to ensure on-time delivery (6 weeks).

Business Impact

Real-time Reporting speed (down from 3 days)
15hrs Manual labor saved per week
